Original Digital Prototype

The original digital prototype designed for those who are sort of feeling hard to decide what to eat for dinner is existing as a platform of a website where there is an interactive lottery prepared for users. The lottery interaction is a series of food images rolling in the frame. Once right click the button, it will randomly stop at a certain image. Consequently, the answer of what to eat tonight is going to be clear to users.

Take a look at the origin

Ideation

The concept of Internet Yami-yichi is to make the digital objects physically exist in the real world. To achieve a physical rendition of an object from my previous digital prototype, I decided to create a hand-made turnplate with a combination of a dispaly board that shows different options of meal. The target audience is still those who are kind of confused of what they are going to eat at dinner.


The origin, which is an interactive lottery digitally displayed to audience, now has been shifted to an physical turnplate of which the inspiration is derived from the digital lottery. The principle of this turnplate is similar to the original interaction, which is randomly choosing the food option.


The entire physical prototype has two separate parts, which are a hand-made turnplate and a display board with food options on it. The audience can turn the wheel with the finger then it will finally stop at a certain place where there is a representative color, the user should subsequently check the display board to find out the food option refered to the color. For example, if the wheel stop at the green section then the chosen food is the pizza with a green background displayed on the board.
